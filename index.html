About the bakery

A bakery shaped by passion

Pete's Bread is a micro bakery in the beautiful Causeway Coast area of Northern Ireland, specialising in nutritious, naturally-leavened, country-style sourdough bread. Our bakes are available exclusively to guests at Peatlands, Causeway Coast.

The process

Bread worth waiting for

Pete's Bread sourdough is made from a blend of speciality flours, water and sea salt — no artificial ingredients, flour improvers or preservatives.

Each loaf takes around 32 hours to make. They're hand-shaped, proofed slowly in wood pulp bannetons and baked in a stone oven to a golden brown crust.

The long, slow fermentation process produces a tastier, more digestible loaf with a wild open crumb.

Real Bread

Pete's Bread is REAL sourdough — just flour, water and sea salt. No additives. No industrial yeast. Pete's Bread is a member of the Real Bread Campaign.

The baker

Meet Pete

Pete Hathaway is a self-taught baker who has been running his micro bakery and supplying his local community since 2021. He specialises in making country-style sourdough bread using speciality flours.

Pete's Bread was established in Oxford, England, when Pete's passion for baking sourdough for family and friends grew into a small business — delivering bread, patisserie and sweet treats around the neighbourhood by bike every Saturday morning, from a small 2.5m wooden cabin at the bottom of the garden.

In 2022, Pete, his family and his microbakery relocated to the Causeway Coast, Northern Ireland. His signature sourdough, The Peatlands Loaf, won a Gold award at the 2022 World Bread Awards.

Pete's sourdough and patisserie is now available exclusively to guests at Peatlands Causeway Coast.
